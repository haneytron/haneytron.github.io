Launching an MVP

David Haney
May 2025

Setting The Stage

2022 Q4:

  • LLMs begin their meteoric rise in popularity

2023 Q1:

  • Stack Overflow LOBs seriously disrupted
  • ↓↓↓ Q&A Traffic
  • ↓↓↓ Teams renewals & revenue

2023 Q2:

  • Response formulated w/ C-Suite and Product partners
  • Launch AI features in late July 2023 at WAD World Conf in Berlin
  • I am appointed by CTO & C-Suite to create & execute Project SAIL (Stack AI Learnings)

The Challenge

  • We need to stop the bleeding - FAST
  • It's May 1, WAD is happening July 27th
  • We have ~3 months to build something great

First: Scope Project SAIL

C-Suite & Board Expectations

  • Top company priority
  • 8-9 working AI prototypes to compete with OpenAI, GitHub, & Glean
  • 2x weekly C-Suite updates on progress

Assemble SAIL Team

  • Work with CTO (Jody) & Director of Product (Ellen) to figure out "skeleton crew" for existing LOBs
  • Keep ~30 on LOBs (Dev + Ops mostly)
  • Take ~20 for SAIL (Dev + Data Science)

Selection Criteria

  • Largely best & brightest - need to hit ground running
  • Balance US and EU staffing for more coverage
  • Include our 3 strongest data scientists w/ demonstrable LLM knowledge as SMEs
  • Include our 2 strongest designers

Establish Goals & Alignment, Expectations, Plan

Goals & Alignment

  • Publish: canonical and clear goals in one place
  • ID key decision makers: Eng (me), Product (Ellen), Data (Michael) - buck stops with us, we're accountable
  • Motivate with WHY: LOB disruption, need to recover revenue & compete with new Q&A paradigm
  • Motivate with WHAT: revealed on world stage July 27, 12k attendees, let's change the world!

Expectations

  • No one gets stuck: raise blockers IMMEDIATELY as they happen
  • Rapid decisions: key folks prioritize solving
  • Default public: over-communicate, solicit constant feedback, test internally often, talk to other SAIL teams
  • Move fast & break things: minimum docs and ceremony, if meetings aren't useful kill them, honor failure, etc.

Plan

  • Now: 2 weeks of rapid R&D prototyping, self-organizing teams, experiment, prototype possible AI features / tools / products.
  • End of week 2: demo everything, cut list down to top n, form teams around them
  • Weeks 3-12: rapid CI/CD, daily quick stand-ups, weekly demos and feedback

Weeks 1-2

  • SAIL Teams: innovate, prototype, create ideas and execute
  • In parallel: I teach myself LLMs, deep dive, can't lead w/o understanding

End of Week 2

  • Teams present ideas: ~8 total
  • I rank them by effort vs expected payoff
  • Present to C-Suite: 5 things are great, 3 are weak, recommend doing 5 things well vs 8 things poorly (narrow our focus, quality over quantity)
  • They agree, prioritize functional prototypes over PoCs and mock-ups

Week 3

  • Bring final 5 ideas to SAIL, assign a Team Lead to each (based on who came up with the ideas + strengths), ask teams to self-organize 3-5 per idea
  • Designers + Data Science cross-functional & on-demand
  • Everyone knows what to do and why: all systems go!

Weeks 3 - 8: Build

  • Teams normalize, largely working well, address personnel and product issues on-the-fly
  • Regular stand-ups and weekly demos to stakeholders
  • Feature pivots as we realize what will work and what won't given time constraints

Weeks 8 - 12: Cut & Polish

  • Aggressively cut nice-to-haves to ensure core functionality gets polish given time constraints
  • New threat: GitHub Copilot reaches escape velocity, realize we can't compete
  • Response: sunset IDE plugin at minimal state, re-allocate team to other 4

1 Week Before WAD

  • Review & ghost-write large parts of CEO's WAD presentation
  • Feature prep & dry-runs in static env for live demos (and recorded backups)

WAD!

Stack Overflow: Community and AI

What I Learned

  • Deadline driven development is difficult
  • Building trust with teams is the key to success
  • Getting products and features in front of users ASAP saves time and money
  • Empower those who are closest to the problems to solve them as SMEs
  • Recognize failure quickly, learn from it, and be blameless
